Scientific references

Every calculation running behind the scenes is based on the following research and official reports:

  1. BMR equation

    Mifflin, M. D., St Jeor, S. T., Hill, L. A., Scott, B. J., Daugherty, S. A., & Koh, Y. O. (1990). A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy individuals. The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 51(2), 241–247.

    doi.org/10.1093/ajcn/51.2.241 ↗
  2. Protein (g/kg)

    Jäger, R., Kerksick, C. M., Campbell, B. I., Cribb, P. J., Wells, S. D., Skwiat, T. M., ... & Antonio, J. (2017). International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: protein and exercise. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 14(1), 20.

    doi.org/10.1186/s12970-017-0177-8 ↗
  3. Activity multipliers (TDEE)

    Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, World Health Organization, & United Nations University. (2004). Human energy requirements: Report of a Joint FAO/WHO/UNU Expert Consultation (FAO Food and Nutrition Technical Report Series No. 1). FAO.

    fao.org/3/y5686e ↗
  4. Fat share & fiber

    Institute of Medicine. (2005). Dietary Reference Intakes for energy, carbohydrate, fiber, fat, fatty acids, cholesterol, protein, and amino acids. The National Academies Press.

    doi.org/10.17226/10490 ↗
  5. Water needs

    EFSA Panel on Dietetic Products, Nutrition and Allergies (NDA). (2010). Scientific Opinion on Dietary Reference Values for water. EFSA Journal, 8(3), 1459.

    doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2010.1459 ↗
  6. Calorie deficit

    Helms, E. R., Aragon, A. A., & Fitschen, P. J. (2014). Evidence-based recommendations for natural bodybuilding contest preparation: Nutrition and supplementation. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 11(1), 20.

    doi.org/10.1186/1550-2783-11-20 ↗
  7. Calorie surplus

    Iraki, J., Fitschen, P., Espinar, S., & Helms, E. (2019). Nutrition recommendations for bodybuilders in the off-season: A narrative review. Sports, 7(7), 154.

    doi.org/10.3390/sports7070154 ↗

These calculations apply to healthy adult populations. If you are pregnant, have a chronic condition or special dietary needs, consult a health professional.
